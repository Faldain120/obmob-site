Lorem sections follow so there is something to scroll under the sticky bar. The oral exam is a three-part examination administered by ABOG, structured around your case list and standardized structured cases across the content blueprint.

Candidates present and defend their own collected cases; examiners probe management decisions, timing, and the reasoning behind them rather than recall alone.

Rehearsing out loud is the part most candidates skip, because it needs another physician in the room. That is the gap an always-available voice examiner fills.

Each drill returns a scored rubric across knowledge, differential, judgment, management, communication, and delivery — plus the moments where the answer wavered.
